Independent Computational Research for Global Digital Governance.

The Berlin Institute for Digital Strategy (BIDS) is a research-led initiative dedicated to the systematic auditing of digital norms and geopolitical friction. Operating at the intersection of International Relations and Computational Social Science, we provide evidence-based insights through large-scale policy monitoring and relational data modeling.

Research Initiatives

Ongoing Monitoring | Digital Sovereignty The "Bandung Spirit" in Cyberspace: Leveraging NAM for Digital Norm-Setting

Utilizing NLP-driven policy auditing, this initiative tracks the discursive alignment between China and the Non-Aligned Movement (NAM). [cite: 1, 390] Our research identifies how anti-hegemonic rhetoric is institutionalized to reshape the global digital order. [cite: 391, 392]

Initially Funded by: Italian Ministry of University and Research (PRIN 2022)
Comparative Audit | Tech-Geopolitics Structural Alignments in Global Technology Ecosystems

A systematic audit of regulatory friction in non-Western markets. We analyze the evolving logics of "Data Sovereignty" and their impact on international digital trade and infrastructure.

Research Support: BIDS Independent Methodology Seed
Archived | Climate & Emerging Economies Climate Financing Policy Auditing in BASIC Countries

Policy-oriented research focusing on the cooperation of climate financing among major emerging economies. This project established the foundation for BIDS's comparative auditing methodology.

Funded by: China Clean Development Mechanism Fund (CCDMF)

Institutional Methodology

Computational Policy Auditing

BIDS maintains proprietary Python-based pipelines for multi-lingual policy extraction. We automate the detection of semantic shifts in official digital strategy documents across jurisdictions.

Network Analysis (SNA)

Utilizing relational data modeling to map institutional interdependencies and the diffusion of digital norms within international organizations.
